9. YOUR RIGHTS

Pursuant to GDPR and Chapter IV of the Personal Information Protection Law of the People’s Republic of China, you have the right to ask each Information Processors or Joint Processors:

  • to access and duplicate your Personal Information;
  • to receive the copy of the Personal Information you provided us (so-called “data portability”) and to have data transmitted to another processor in compliance with regulations of the state cyberspace administration;
  • the rectification of the Personal Information in your possession;
  • the erasure of any Personal Information in relation to which we no longer have any legal basis for processing;
  • to withdraw your consent;
  • request for interpretation of this notice;
  • the limitation of the way in which we process your Personal Information, within the limits set by the applicable law data protection law;
  • and any other rights you may have under applicable law.

Right to object: in addition to the rights listed above, you always have the right to object at any time to the processing of your Personal Information carried out by the Information Processor or Joint Processor for the pursuit of its legitimate interest. You have the right to object to direct marketing, which includes profiling. If you prefer that the processing of your Personal Information is carried out solely through traditional contact methods, you can object to the processing of your Personal Information carried out through automated contact methods.

You also have the right to withdraw, in whole or in part, the consent to the processing of Personal Information concerning you for the purpose of sending advertisements or direct selling or for carrying out market research or commercial communication with automated contact methods (e-mail, other remote communication systems via communication networks such as, for instance: SMS, MMS, messaging platforms, etc.) and traditional contact methods (mail).

If you no longer need to use any product or service provided by the Information Processor or Joint Processor, you have the right to de-register your account. After the de-registration, the Information Processor or Joint Processor shall erasure all of your Personal Information.

The exercise of these rights, which can be done through the contact details indicated in section 1, is not subject to formal constraints. In the event that you exercise any of the above mentioned rights, it will be the responsibility of the Information Processor or Joint Processor that you contacted to verify if you are entitled to exercise the right and to provide you with an answer, normally within half a month.
